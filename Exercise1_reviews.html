    Name - Harry Potter and Philosopher Stone

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    J.K. Rowling's debut novel, "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (known as "Sorcerer's Stone" in the US), introduces readers to a magical world that has captivated audiences for decades. The story follows Harry Potter, an orphaned boy who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. Whisked away from his neglectful aunt and uncle, Harry enters the hidden world of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Rowling's vivid imagination brings to life a richly detailed magical universe, complete with its own history, customs, and quirky characters. The book cleverly blends elements of fantasy, mystery, and coming-of-age tales, appealing to readers of all ages. As Harry navigates his new life, he forms close friendships with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, faces the challenges of magical education, and uncovers the truth about his parents' deaths and his connection to the dark wizard Voldemort. The plot builds to an exciting climax as Harry and his friends confront danger and evil, showcasing themes of courage, friendship, and the power of love. While the writing style is straightforward and accessible for younger readers, Rowling weaves in enough complexity and hints at a larger narrative to keep older audiences engaged. The book's success lies in its ability to create a sense of wonder and possibility, inviting readers to imagine themselves in this magical world. "Philosopher's Stone" sets the foundation for a series that would go on to become a cultural phenomenon, introducing beloved characters and magical concepts that continue to enchant new generations of readers.

    Review -

    France, 1916. Europe is riven by two years of mechanised slaughter in the Great War. Hundreds of miles of muddy trenches and chemically-polluted wasteland run from the North Sea to Switzerland. Here at the southern border, so-called Kilometre Zero is quiet. The enlisted men are too cold and tired to pursue valour, the officers too wary of upsetting their neutral Swiss neighbours. Livestock are found mutilated, a night patrol suffers a grisly encounter, and a captured German deserter carries a dire warning. As the full moon rises above the Jura Mountains, American legionnaire Sam Carter and British officer Lucas Avery once again find themselves drawn into battle with a supernatural evil...

    Name - 12 Rules for Life

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    What does everyone in the modern world need to know? Renowned psychologist Jordan B. Peterson's answer to this most difficult of questions uniquely combines the hard-won truths of ancient tradition with the stunning revelations of cutting-edge scientific research.     Name - Rich Dad Poor Dad

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    "Rich Dad Poor Dad" is a personal finance book written by Robert Kiyosaki. The book was first published in 1997 and has since become a bestseller, with over 40 million copies sold worldwide. The book is written in a conversational tone and is aimed at anyone looking to gain financial literacy and independence. The book is divided into ten chapters, each of which focuses on a different aspect of personal finance. The author begins by introducing the two main characters in the book, his "rich dad" and his "poor dad." The "rich dad" is the father of his best friend, who is a successful entrepreneur and investor, while the "poor dad" is his own biological father, who is an educated man but struggles financially. Throughout the book, the author draws on the lessons he learned from both his "rich dad" and "poor dad" to offer practical advice on how to achieve financial success. He discusses topics such as the importance of financial education, the difference between assets and liabilities, and the power of passive income. One of the book's main themes is financial literacy's importance. The author argues that many people struggle financially because they lack the basic knowledge necessary to manage their money effectively. He encourages readers to take an active role in their financial education and to seek out resources that can help them learn more about personal finance. A key concept highlighted in the book is the distinction between assets and liabilities. As per the author's definition, assets refer to anything that generates income or adds value to your finances, whereas liabilities are anything that incurs expenses or reduces your net worth. He encourages readers to focus on acquiring assets and minimizing their liabilities, as this is the key to building wealth.

    Name - A Teaspoon of Earth and Sea

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    Growing up in a small rice-farming village in 1980s Iran, eleven-year-old Saba Hafezi and her twin sister, Mahtab, are captivated by America. They keep lists of English words and collect illegal Life magazines, television shows, and rock music. So when her mother and sister disappear, leaving Saba and her father alone in Iran, Saba is certain that they have moved to America without her. But her parents have taught her that “all fate is written in the blood,” and that twins will live the same life, even if separated by land and sea. As she grows up in the warmth and community of her local village, falls in and out of love, and struggles with the limited possibilities in post-revolutionary Iran, Saba envisions that there is another way for her story to unfold. Somewhere, it must be that her sister is living the Western version of this life. And where Saba’s world has all the grit and brutality of real life under the new Islamic regime, her sister’s experience gives her a freedom and control that Saba can only dream of.

    Name - Carlo Ancelloti Quiet Leadership

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    Carlo Ancelotti's "Quiet Leadership" offers a refreshing perspective on management in the high-pressure world of professional football. Drawing from his extensive experience as a player and coach at elite clubs, Ancelotti presents a leadership philosophy that emphasizes calm, respect, and emotional intelligence over authoritarian tactics. The book provides insights into Ancelotti's approach to handling star players, navigating club politics, and maintaining composure in the face of intense media scrutiny. Ancelotti's anecdotes from his time at clubs like AC Milan, Chelsea, and Real Madrid illustrate his principles in action, offering readers a behind-the-scenes look at top-level football management. He stresses the importance of adaptability, clear communication, and creating a positive team culture. The author's humble and reflective tone adds credibility to his advice, as he openly discusses both successes and failures. While the book is primarily focused on football, many of Ancelotti's leadership lessons are applicable to management in other fields. He emphasizes the value of listening, empathy, and building trust with team members. The book's structure, alternating between personal stories and leadership lessons, makes for an engaging read. However, some readers might find certain sections repetitive or wish for more in-depth tactical analysis. Overall, "Quiet Leadership" offers a valuable perspective on effective management in high-stakes environments, presenting a thoughtful alternative to more aggressive leadership styles often associated with successful coaches. It's a must-read for football enthusiasts and aspiring leaders alike.

    Name - Abdul Kalam Wings of Fire

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    "Wings of Fire," the autobiography of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, co-authored with Arun Tiwari, is a compelling narrative that traces the journey of a humble boy from Rameswaram to becoming India's "Missile Man" and eventually the country's 11th President. The book, divided into four sections, offers a candid and inspiring account of Kalam's life, blending personal anecdotes with India's scientific and technological progress. Kalam's writing style is simple yet profound, making complex scientific concepts accessible to the general reader. He vividly describes his childhood, the influence of his parents and teachers, and his early career at DRDO and ISRO. The narrative skillfully intertwines Kalam's personal growth with India's emerging space and defense programs, offering unique insights into the challenges and triumphs of Indian science. Kalam's unwavering patriotism, his vision for India's technological self-reliance, and his emphasis on the power of dreams and perseverance shine throughout the book. He doesn't shy away from discussing failures and setbacks, presenting them as stepping stones to success. The book's title metaphorically represents Kalam's rise from humble beginnings, much like a rocket launching into space. While some readers might find certain technical details challenging, the overall narrative remains engaging and inspirational. "Wings of Fire" is more than just an autobiography; it's a testament to the power of education, hard work, and visionary thinking. It continues to inspire generations of Indians, especially youth, to dream big and contribute to nation-building, making it a timeless classic in Indian literature

    Name - A Man Called Ove

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    Fredrik Backman's "A Man Called Ove" is a heartwarming and humorous tale that explores the depths of human connection and the power of community. The novel introduces us to Ove, a curmudgeonly 59-year-old widower whose gruff exterior masks a heart broken by loss and a life shaped by principles of order and routine. As the story unfolds, Backman skillfully peels back the layers of Ove's character, revealing a complex man whose rigidity is challenged by the chaos of his vibrant, multicultural neighbors. The narrative alternates between Ove's present-day interactions and flashbacks to his past, gradually unveiling the experiences that molded him. This structure allows readers to understand and empathize with Ove, even as his cantankerous behavior initially repels those around him. Backman's writing shines in its ability to find humor in everyday situations while tackling serious themes of grief, loneliness, and the search for purpose in life. As Ove reluctantly becomes entangled in his neighbors' lives, the story evolves into a beautiful exploration of how community can heal and transform even the most isolated individuals. The supporting characters are well-drawn and diverse, each bringing their own quirks and challenges that ultimately enrich Ove's life. Backman's prose is simple yet evocative, infused with dry wit and moments of profound emotion. While the plot may occasionally veer into sentimentality, the author's genuine affection for his characters keeps the story grounded and relatable. "A Man Called Ove" is a touching, funny, and ultimately life-affirming novel that reminds us of the importance of human connection and the unexpected joys that can arise from opening our hearts to others.

    Name - Good Company

    Rating - ⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Review -

    Flora Mancini has been happily married for more than twenty years. But everything she thought she knew about herself, her marriage, and her relationship with her best friend, Margot, is upended when she stumbles upon an envelope containing her husband’s wedding ring—the one he claimed he lost one summer when their daughter, Ruby, was five.
